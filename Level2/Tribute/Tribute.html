Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

The People's President & Missile Man of India

Visionary Leader

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he became one of India's most beloved leaders.

Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology, Dr. Kalam was a scientist, teacher, and inspiration to millions of young minds across the nation.

Life Timeline

October 15, 1931
Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, into a humble fishing family. His father Jainulabdeen was a boat owner and imam, while his mother Ashiamma was a housewife.
1954
Graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and moved to Madras to study aerospace engineering at Madras Institute of Technology.
1969
Moved to ISRO where he served as project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite.
1980s-1990s
Led the development of multiple successful missiles including Agni and Prithvi, earning him the title "Missile Man of India."
1998
Played a pivotal role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests, establishing India as a nuclear power.
2002-2007
Served as the 11th President of India, becoming known as the "People's President" for his humility and accessibility.
July 27, 2015
Passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong, continuing his mission of inspiring young minds until his last breath.

Major Achievements

Scientific Contributions

Pioneered India's missile technology, leading projects like SLV-III, Agni, and Prithvi missiles. His work significantly advanced India's defense capabilities and space program.

Presidential Legacy

Served as the 11th President of India (2002-2007), known for his humility, accessibility, and dedication to connecting with young people across the nation.

Educational Impact

Authored over 25 books, delivered countless lectures, and inspired millions of students to pursue science and technology with passion and dedication.

Awards & Recognition

Recipient of India's highest civilian honors including Padma Bhushan (1981), Padma Vibhushan (1990), and Bharat Ratna (1997).

Nuclear Program

Chief scientific adviser during India's nuclear tests at Pokhran-II in 1998, establishing India as a declared nuclear weapons state.

Youth Inspiration

Dedicated his post-presidency life to teaching and inspiring young minds, believing that youth are the key to India's bright future.
